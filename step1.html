Step 1: Docking (local)

Docking study between ligand (PF-07321332) \bb{ligand.pdb} and receptor (3CL$^{\rm pro}$, domain I+II), \bb{3clc.pdb} is performed using Autodock4\cite{Autodock} with the script \bb{docking.bash}. \begin{alltt} {\bf{docking.bash -l ligand -r 3clc -x -10.55 -y 20.65 -z 66.75 -p 60}} \end{alltt} The wrapper needs as mandatory arguments the ligand and receptor pdb files (with no extension). In the example above we have specified the coordinates of the box center as those of corresponding to the midpoint of a vector connecting the S(CYS145) and NE(HIS41) atoms of the 3CLpro catalytic dyad. The calculation runs 50 docking experiments using the Lamarckian genetic algorithm with the center of mass of the fully flexible ligand placed within a 22.5 \AA~ (60 grid points, \textbf{-p 60}, spaced 0.375 {\AA}) side-length cubic box centered at the receptor active site (\textbf{-x -10.55 -y 20.65 -z 66.75}). Up to 2.5 million energy evaluations are performed for each experiment. The results are clustered using a tolerance of 2.0 \AA. The lowest energy docked conformation of the lowest energy cluster is the ``best-docked" conformation. The complex structure of ligand (``best-docked") and protein, \br{complex\_ligand.pdb\_OUT}, is the starting structure for HPC\_Drug. For help about docking script, just issue the previous command without arguments ({\bf{docking.bash}}).
